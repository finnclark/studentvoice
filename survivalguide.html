Community

Be Part of the Community

No doubt at some point you’ve thought that upon enrolling at university you are also signing up for an experience that will remain with you for the rest of your life – that your time there is not only a time to gear up for a career, but to also kit out the memory bank with a few fond memories and inspiring stories. To learn and to grow as an individual.

Let us tell you now that while this is absolutely true, there is one crucial factor that doesn’t seem to get mentioned so much: if you truly want to get the most out of your time here – then the only person that can make that happen is you. Classes are good to gain the skills, but there’s more to life than knowing what’s what. There’s a bigger picture being put together and you are but one small part of it. You need to talk, you need to meet people. You need to make the effort to do this now more than ever before. It’s true, some people find this alot easier than others. But somewhere along the lines we’re all in the same boat.

Be part of the community here. There’s more to learning than taking notes. Learning to communitcate well will have an overflow effect into everything you do.

Taking Critique

We know that it’s hard to wear your heart on your sleeve and open your work up to others for feedback. You also may find it hard to put others in this position, but learning to take critique is so important. It’s not a personal attack on you, it’s there to help you grow and mature as a designer. Engage in this and you will be better, we promise.

Share Your Ideas - They'll Be Better

Here’s the thing: people get so precious about a good idea as if anyone at any minute might catch wind and steal it. Yes... it does happen, but lurking behind that one idea are plenty more, and they’re all better than the last. Don’t live in fear. The more you share and express your ideas, the more your ideas will change things for the better.

Showing Work

This kind of sums up the last two points. What good is your work if you wont let people see it? It doesn’t matter if it’s good or bad, we’re all learning as we go. Even the best designers look back at work and cringe. Exhibiting work should become part of you – share your gift.

Grades

The Truth About Grades

No doubt you want to do well here, and as such arming yourself with a set of straight A’s is something to aspire to. While there is nothing wrong with this, it shouldn’t be the sole factor that drives your work.

The Brief vs. The Idea

Lessons here are designed to train you in a specific skill or task. To do this requiers a certain framework in order to measure how you as a student have interpreted these lessons. Trust us when we say, that your tutor would much prefer helping you unpack a good idea, than hold your hand though the brief.

This is not NCEA

With the above paragraph in mind you need to fully understand, how much this is not NCEA. There are no exemplars, no learning guides. When you’re done here, the person on the other side of the desk interviewing you doesn’t care about your grades, they care about the work you can do. Grades are a guide, but they’re not everything.
